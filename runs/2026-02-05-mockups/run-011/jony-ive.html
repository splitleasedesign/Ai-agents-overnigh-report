Implementation Notes: Approach B - Internal Trigger Connection

Key Eyal Principles Applied:

  1. Action Center - EYAL p.41: "External triggers are embedded with information which tells the user what to do next."
  2. Time-Decay Badges - EYAL p.17: "Habits are like pearls... continuous presence of an irritant"
  3. Earnings Display - EYAL p.48: "Create an association with a person's pain... source of relief"
  4. Performance Metrics - EYAL p.79: "Variable rewards are one of the most powerful tools"
  5. Activity Feed - EYAL p.79: "Feedback loops... predictable ones don't create desire"
  6. Push Opt-In - EYAL p.44: "Without owned triggers... must spend to acquire users"
  7. Referral Card - EYAL p.43: "Relationship triggers can create viral hyper-growth"
  8. Co-Host Banner - EYAL p.44: "Owned triggers consume real estate in user's environment"
  9. Status Summary - EYAL p.48: "Emotions, particularly negative ones, are powerful internal triggers"

Internal Triggers Addressed:

Emotion Trigger Question Design Response
Anxiety "Did I miss something?" Action Center (aggregated triggers)
Hope "Am I earning?" Earnings Card ($2,450 + trend)
Pride "Am I a good host?" Performance Metrics (95%, 4.9, Top 10%)
Fear "Is something wrong?" Status Summary ("All listings healthy")
Curiosity "What's happening?" Activity Feed (variable content)

Habit Formation Prediction:

  • Cycles to internal trigger: 3-5 weeks of daily exposure
  • Target association: "When I feel anxious about my rentals, Split Lease makes me feel better"
  • Sustainability: High - emotional connection creates strong habit loop

  • Action buttons stack vertically on small screens
