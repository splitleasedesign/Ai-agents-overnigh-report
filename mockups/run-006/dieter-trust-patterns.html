Trust Patterns and Error States

Honest design decisions for Split Lease legal documents, payments, and error handling. Every pattern here passes the "would users feel tricked?" test.

1

Legal Document Trust Patterns

Legal documents are intimidating. Progressive disclosure reduces cognitive load while respecting the user's right to read everything. The "Request Changes" button has equal visual weight to "Sign" - negotiation is legitimate, not discouraged.

Honest vs Dishonest Payment Copy

Do Not Use

"You're OVERDUE! Pay immediately to avoid consequences."

"WARNING: Your account is at risk!"

"Act now before late fees apply!"

Use This Instead

"Your payment of $1,850 was due on February 15."

"Please complete payment or contact us if you need help."

"Having trouble? We can discuss options."

Error State Patterns

Errors should guide without blaming. State what happened, assign responsibility appropriately (our error vs external), explain what happens next, and provide clear action paths. Never catastrophize - most errors are recoverable.

Payment couldn't be processed

Your payment of $1,850.00 did not go through. Here's what we know:

What happened
  • Reason: Card declined - insufficient funds
  • Card ending in 4242
Please resolve within 48 hours to avoid booking issues.
Need more time? Let us know

What this means
  • Your original dates remain confirmed: March 7-9, 2026

  • You can submit a new request if you'd still like to explore date changes

Dark Pattern Prevention Checklist

Run this checklist against any new Leases page design. Each item tests a specific manipulation pattern. A passing score requires all items to pass - there's no "acceptable" level of dark patterns.

Document Signing

  • No forced scroll-to-bottom before signing
  • "Request Changes" has equal weight to "Sign"
  • No countdown timer on signing deadline
  • No "Only 2 people viewing" scarcity
  • PDF download always available

Payment Reminders

  • No red styling before payment is actually late
  • No false late fee threats
  • No multiple pop-ups for same due payment
  • "Pay later" option not hidden
  • Contact support option visible

Date Change Tool (DCT)

  • No large countdown timer creating anxiety
  • No interrupting pop-ups about expiration
  • No "You're about to lose $X!" framing
  • Clear warning before auto-decline at expiration
  • Savings show trade-off (date change required)

Flexibility Scores

  • No "Your score is dropping!" warnings
  • No comparative shaming ("bottom 20%")
  • No gamification (streaks, levels, points)
  • Measures responsiveness, not compliance
  • Criteria is transparent and published
