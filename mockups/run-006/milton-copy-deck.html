Tone Guidelines Reference

One voice, context shifts. Host and Guest get the same tone, different framing.

Voice Principles

Principle Application Example
First person plural for Split Lease actionsUse "we" when Split Lease is the actor"We'll notify you when it's ready"
Second person for user actionsUse "you/your" for what they do"Your payment is due Feb 3"
Active voice, present tenseDirect and immediate"Payment is processing" not "Payment is being processed"
Contractions are fineNatural speech"don't", "we'll", "you're"
Exclamation points only for celebrationsReserve for wins"Lease signed!" but "Payment due Feb 3" (no exclamation)

Host vs Guest Framing

Scenario Host Sees Guest Sees
Payment reminder"Payment from Sarah is due on Feb 3""Your payment to John is due on Feb 3"
Document status"Awaiting Sarah's signature""Your signature is needed"
Date change"Sarah has requested a date change""You requested a date change"
Booking confirmed"You have a new booking!""Your stay is confirmed!"
Request expired"A request from Maria expired""Your request has expired"

Money Conversation Tone

Do
"Payment due Feb 3"
Don't
"You owe $2,400"
Do
"Due in 3 days (Feb 3)"
Don't
"Due soon"
Do
"Payment was due on Feb 1"
Don't
"Your payment is OVERDUE"
Do
"Set up auto-pay — we'll handle the rest"
Don't
"Set up auto-pay to never miss a payment"

Legal-Friendly Balance

Do — Lead with human, follow with legal
"Your lease includes a 30-day notice period. (See Section 4.2 for details.)"
Don't — Legal-first
"Pursuant to Section 4.2 of your agreement, a 30-day notice period applies."
Do — Reassurance
"To keep everything running smoothly, please sign by Feb 15."
Don't — Intimidation
"Failure to sign by Feb 15 will result in cancellation of your booking."

Error & Failure Tone

Do — Neutral language
"Payment couldn't be processed"
Don't — Blame language
"Your payment failed"
Do — Factual status
"Past Due"
Don't — Judgmental
"Late" or "Overdue"
Do — Neutral expiration
"This request has expired"
Don't — Blame assignment
"Your host didn't respond in time"

Key Patterns Summary

# Pattern Description
1Traffic Light StatusesGreen (complete), Amber (action needed), Red (critical) — universal and instant
2Specificity Over Urgency"Due Feb 3" beats "Due soon" — dates are more urgent than adjectives
3Lead with Win, Follow with Detail"Lease signed — you're all set!" then the explanation
4Neutral Failures"Couldn't be processed" not "failed" — no blame, just facts
5One Voice, Context ShiftsSame tone for Host and Guest, different framing by role
6Legal in ParenthesesPlain language first, legal reference available but not leading
7Money is DirectSpecific amounts and dates, never vague ("your payment")
8Empty States EducateEvery empty state explains what will appear and why
